<h1><strong>Port of Colón Multi-Week Analysis</strong></h1>

<h2><strong>Executive Summary</strong></h2>

Analyzed the Port of Colón’s connectivity, infrastructure capacity, technological adoption, and sustainability performance using multi-week presentations and a full research report. The project assessed how the port maintains competitiveness as a major Caribbean transshipment hub.

<h2><strong>Business Problems</strong></h2>

Evaluate whether the Port of Colón is operationally ready for future growth amid increasing vessel sizes, sustainability requirements, and competition from regional ports.

<h2><strong>Key Questions</strong></h2>

•⁠  ⁠How strong is Colón’s global and inland connectivity?  
•⁠  ⁠Can the port handle modern vessel sizes and rising throughput?  
•⁠  ⁠How much do PORTCEL & CICO improve speed and efficiency?  
•⁠  ⁠Is the port advancing sustainably and competitively?

<h2><strong>Methodology</strong></h2>

•⁠  ⁠Reviewed TEU throughput, vessel capacity, berth depth, and layout.  
•⁠  ⁠Assessed digital systems (PORTCEL, CICO) and their impact on clearance and turnaround time.  
•⁠  ⁠Evaluated environmental, economic, and social sustainability factors.  
•⁠  ⁠Compared Colón with Cartagena across TEU volume, cost, and technology.
